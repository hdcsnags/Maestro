# Task 002 — Direction Memo: what should Maestro do next?

You are an independent senior technical advisor. You have no history with this
project and no stake in any prior decision.

## Context in this folder

1. `context-philosophy.md` — the project's thesis and product vision, written
   by its builder.
2. `context-state.md` — the operational state file: what works, what's broken,
   what's in flight, and the full recent session log.
3. `context-graph-report.md` — an AST-derived knowledge-graph report of the
   codebase (communities, coupling, structural findings).

Read all three. Form your own view of where this project actually is — the
documents may disagree with each other or oversell; weigh them as evidence,
not gospel.

## Your deliverable (markdown, printed to stdout)

A position memo answering one question: **what is the single
highest-leverage focus for this project over the next six weeks?**

Structure:

1. **Where the project actually is** — your independent read, in a short
   paragraph. Call out anything the documents claim that the evidence does not
   support.
2. **The bet** — the one focus you would commit to, stated plainly. Explain
   what it wins and why now.
3. **What you are explicitly deferring** — name the attractive things you
   would NOT do yet, and what deferring them costs.
4. **Risks of your bet and mitigations** — the ways your chosen direction
   fails, and what you'd do about each.
5. **First three concrete steps** — specific enough that work could start
   tomorrow.

Rules: commit to ONE position — no "it depends" hedging, no portfolio of
options. There is no correct answer; you are being evaluated on judgment, not
compliance. Do not modify or create any files; do not read files outside this
folder; print the memo to stdout only. Length: whatever the memo needs — no
padding.
